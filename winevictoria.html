Australian Wine Overview


According to the 2018 report by International Organisation of Vine and Wine, Australia is currently the seventh largest wine manufacturer in the world, producing more than 1000 million litres of wine every year. Here is a sneak peek into the major contributions of states in Australia.

  • South Australia has the most wineries out of all the states.
  • Tasminia has the fewest wineries but has the highest average ratings.

Victoria is the second smallest state but is home to many prestigious wine regions and individual wineries in Australia. It also has the greatest diversity of regional and site climates, which allows for the production of virtually every imaginable wine style from fine sparkling wine, high quality Chardonnay and Pinot Noir, age-worthy Cabernet, savoury Shiraz and the historic fortified wines of Rutherglen.

Overall, Victoria has ranked

  • No.2 in terms of wine production scale measured by the number of wineries;
  • No.3 in terms of ratings given by sommeliers.

Note: as the dataset grows and becomes more complete, ratings of each state may vary. Currently, each state's ratings are very similar.

Victorian Wine Regions Inspection

Highlights of Victoria

In Victoria, there are nine popular wine regions. They are Rutherglen, Heathcote, Yarra Valley, Goulburn Valley, Pyrenees, Grampians, Nagambie Lakes, Mornington Peninsula, and Bendigo. Thirty-four grape varieties are grown in vineyards to make different wine flavors. Vineyards and wineries are scattered around Victoria.

"Victorian wine facts":

  • The wine region has the most wineries: Yarra Valley;
  • The wine region has the highest ratings: Rutherglen;
  • The winery has the highest ratings: Eldridge Estate;
  • The most popular grape variety: Shiraz.

VicWine Data Visualisation Playground Rules

  • The VicWine Data Visualisation Playground is composed of four interactive, linking visualisations. They drill down into more details from the state level (Victoria) to wine regions then to wineries.
    1. Victorian Wine Regions Overview (top left)
    2. Victorian Map of Wineries(purple dots) and Wine Regions(grape icons) (top right)
    3. Grape Varieties Growth Summary (bottom left)
    4. Winery Ranking Chart (bottom right)
  • Visualisation 1 is the main filter for the rest visualisations. Each colorful circle represents a wine region. User can hover over and click them to see more information. After user clicks a circle, the rest visualisations will update correspondingly. Wineries in this selected wine region will be hightlighed on the map. Grape varieties in this region will be drawn. A new list of winery ranking in this region will be displayed.
  • Users can click the circles representing grape varieties in visualisation 3 to find out the wineries that grow each kind of grape after choosing a wine region.
  • Users can click the wineries in visualisation 4 to find their exact locations on the map.

Note: due to the limited space, in state level, top 20 wineries are shown in the ranking list. For each wine region, full list of wineries is displayed.
